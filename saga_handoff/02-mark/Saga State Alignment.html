Should every state be centred?

Playing already grows from the centre. Buffering, downloading and finished currently sit on a shelf (bottom-anchored). Here’s each one both ways — the faint line marks the anchor.

Centred = mid-line anchor
Shelf = floor anchor
Recommendation: centre everything. The mark’s identity is the centred form that folds into the play triangle — so if buffering/finished sit on a shelf, switching states makes the whole mark hop vertically (anchor jumps from floor to mid-line). Centred keeps the optical centre fixed, so any state crossfades into any other with no jump, and they all share one baseline with the wordmark.
The one debatable case is Downloading. “Filling up from the floor” is a strong, literal progress metaphor, so shelf reads slightly clearer there. Centred still works (bars complete and hold), and it keeps the no-jump consistency — but if you want one deliberate exception, downloading is the candidate. My vote is still centred for cohesion.

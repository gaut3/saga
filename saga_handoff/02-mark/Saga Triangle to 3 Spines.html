Both share the exact same play triangle when paused — the only difference is what it unfolds into. Three spines turn the playing button into the brand itself, which is lovely for identity but reads as an equalizer (“something is playing”) more than a button you press to pause. Two bars stay an obvious pause control. A nice middle path: use the 3-spine logo as a non-interactive “now-playing” indicator (mini-player, list rows) and keep the 2-bar morph on the actual transport button — same triangle, two endings for two jobs.
